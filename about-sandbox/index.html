About SANDbox

Self   Advocacy   Network  for  Disability

Sandbox started as an occupational therapy doctoral capstone with the goal of connecting disabled folks to valuable self help resources. 

It can be so frustrating trying to navigate your health as a disabled person. Sometimes it feels like the majority of health resources are speaking over, or speaking to anyone but you! It can feel really hurtful when resources address disabled existence as a task for non-disabled people to manage.

Disabled people deserve a place with dedicated tools and resources for building personal agency in their health and their lives. Sandbox tries to be that place by collecting and vetting materials to fit into a set of guidelines. 

Required Guidelines for Resource ApprovalAdditional Details
Must be written primarily for a disabled audience

Anyone is welcome to use the resources here, but we want resources to talk to disabled people

We don’t want materials that discuss the continued existence of disabled people as something that requires effort from others.

Must feature actionable advice

Memoirs and stories can be interesting and comforting, but these stories are meant to be entertaining. We want our content to focus on increasing agency. 


Grassroots movements are powerful, but what can someone be doing to help themself in the meantime?

Must present information accuratelyInformation should be validated somehow. It could be an own-voices description of a personal experience. It could be written by someone in a professional advice role. It could also be vetted by someone at Sandbox if validation isn’t clear
Must use kind language toward others

Example phrases of hurtful content that we do not allow:

“Heres how to stop chronic pain from driving you crazy”

“At least I’m not in a wheelchair”

Must be free or low-costIt's easy to unintentionally run up a bill on disability care, especially if someone is feeling impulsive or overwhelmed. We don’t want people to feel pressured into purchasing things
