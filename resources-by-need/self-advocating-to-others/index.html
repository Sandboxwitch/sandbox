Self Advocating to Others

SARTAC
The Self Advocacy Resource and Technical Assistance Center offers an expansive library of resources designed to help people self advocate in a variety of situations. Many, but not all of their resources focus on disability.

LDA Guide to Self Advocating
The Learning Disabilities Association of America runs a bi-weekly podcast with information on various learning disabilities, disability supports, and more. Their episode on self advocacy helps provide insight on the disability self advocacy process

When, Why, and How to Disclose your Autism
The Autism Empowerment Podcast has a lot of episodes on disability culture, daily life, and advocacy. This episode in particular explores how to practice safe and effective self-advocacy with a learning disability. It is designed to address Autism specifically, but contains information that will be useful for a variety of needs

Advocating for Myself (Teens)

The Pacer Center has a self advocacy resource library of PDFs written specifically for teen and young adult contexts; Topics include:
  • Navigating a 504 or IEP
  • Advocating as a new adult in college
  • Making the transition to independent living
  • Making a self advocacy checklist
  • What caring for yourself means

Your ADA Rights in Plain Words
The Autistic Self Advocacy Network published a simple guide with pictures about what the ADA means, and what your rights are. This guide was written for people with Autism, but the information contained within can be used for a variety of disabilities
