Employment

Whenever you are advocating within an institution, it is important to remember:

  1. The human resource and disability support staff of an institution exist for the betterment of that institution
  2. It is rare that the staff of an institution will prioritise the betterment of your health more than the comforts of the institution 
  3. It is recommended that people read up on rights and advocacy strategies before discussing your disability with an institution

The resources on this page are unfortunately nearly all USA exclusive, but may contain valuable advice on communication, disclosure and more. 

The Job Accommodation Network
JAN is a network with a massive library of resources about disability experiences and rights in every stage of employment. They have sources on early advocacy, disclosure requirements, USA ADA, job seeking, and employment-related legal advice for people with disabilities
