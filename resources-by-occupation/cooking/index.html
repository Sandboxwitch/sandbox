Accessible Chef

Accessible Chef provides a lot of recipes, guides, tool, and kitchen modifications for cooking and baking with disabilities. All recipes are written with a broad disabled audience in mind, including people with intellectual disabilities. Their large photo guide recipe checklists are helpful for a variety of disabilities including people who are working on organization, memory, visual skills, independence, and more

Sad Bastard's Cookbook (Free PDF)

Authors: Rachel A Rosen and Zilla Novikov

The name Sad Bastards Cookbook may seem a little unusual, but it was written with love and support by disabled authors for disabled readers. This guide is for “spoonies” or people with low energy, chronic pain, brain fog, or other factors that limit the amount of energy one can put into a focused task. Simple recipes, simple ingredients, and effort ratings to help you make nutritious meals. We all have to eat

Good and Cheap (Free PDF)

Author: Leanne Brown

Good and Cheap is a free cookbook filled with recipes that cost $4 or less to make. It was designed for people with budget limitations due to a disability. Recipes range from simple to fancy, with tips and tricks to get the most out of limited budgets, SNAP, food banks and assistance programs

The Elements of Baking

Author: Katarina Cermelj

The Elements of Baking isn't a cookbook in the traditional sense. Its a food chemist's guide on how to modify and convert recipes to be safe for people with specific dietary needs. Baking is chemistry and this book helps you make sure you get the right moisture, rise, densities and more that are required for a recipe to "work". Written to address the following needs: 

  • Egg free
  • Gluten free
  • Dairy Free 
  • Vegan
