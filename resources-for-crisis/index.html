Resources for Crisis 

Each of the numbers below has been vetted by the Don’t Call the Police advocacy network. None of the phone numbers below will share your information with police, or involve law enforcement in your crisis. 

Emergent Crisis Helpline

National Mental Health Crisis Line

Dial 988 from any US Phone, 24/7

The national suicide prevention hotline is a free, confidential support line that can be accessed 24/7. The call center is staffed by trained service providers who can speak with you, as well as refer you to local resources

Eating Disorder Helpline

ANAD Eating Disorder Helpline

(630) 577-1330  M-F 9am - 9pm CST

ANAD is the National Association of Anorexia Nervosa and Associated Disorders. They provide national assistance and can refer to local resources. They focus on a peer support model. If they don't answer right away, you can leave a message. They may take up to 24 hours to return your call

Substance Use Helplines


SAMSHA National Helpline

Dial 988 from any US Phone, 24/7

The Substance Abuse and Mental Health Services Administration of the USA provides a free, confidential line for people managing mental health and substance abuse crises. They can refer you to local support groups, community organizations, and treatment facilities

BIPOC Helpline

Blackline

1-800 604-5841  (24/7)

Blackline is a 24 hour anonymous hotline that provides crisis counseling and recommendations for positive mental health outcomes. Per their website, Blackline caters specifically to the Black, Black LGBTQI, Brown, Native and Muslim community

Phone operators of teen healthline may be legally required by law to report cases of child endangerment. Please proceed with caution. Remember, you never have to disclose personal information over a helpline if you do not feel comfortable doing so.
