Autism Self Advocacy Network 
The Autism Self Advocacy Network is a leader in promoting Autistic voices and health. Their website is packed full of toolkits, free ebooks, community connections, educational resources, and more. They cover topics like understanding your Autism, thriving in college, finding and maintaining a job, housing, voting, and so many more things.

Association for Autism and Neurodiversity
The Association for Autism and Neurodiversity aims to provide education, community and support to Autistic and neurodivergent populations. They have excellent guides on self disclosure in a variety of circumstances. They even have some financial aid available for supporting treatment and quality of life.

ASPIRE 
ASPIRE is a research organization for the topic of Autism. Some of their materials are for caretakers and educators but they also have a dedicated self advocacy section. They have forms and toolkits to empower Autistic people to communicate and thrive in a variety of environments and circumstances

Self Care for Autistic People

Author: Emily Ladau 

This self care guide is filled with many different Autism affirming self care activities. They are designed to prioritize your health alongside concepts like self acceptance, destigmatization, forming community connections, and more. Practical examples, with provided explanations.

Looking After Your Autistic Self

Author: Niamh Garvey 

This book is filled with a lot of practical advice on managing sensory needs, processing emotions, and caring for your Autistic self. It has quick solutions for when you’re struggling, and more thorough strategies for when you have the energy to experiment with and plan around your needs. It really emphasizes adult experiences with Autism rather than focusing on strategies for managing childhood behaviors.

How to Parent like an Autistic

Author: K. Bron Johnson

A guide to being an Autistic parent. The book tackles common needs and even misunderstandings that come up in your journey as an Autistic parent, with your Autistic child. You both have needs and this short book offers insights into how to balance those needs.

Unmasking Autism: Discovering the New Faces of Neurodiversity

Author: Devon Price

 

Every Autistic individual experiences their Autism differently. This book focuses on understanding and caring for your intersectional identities with Autism like gender, race, age, and more. This book priorities concepts of self understanding, self acceptance, and expression. The author is proudly transgender.

Some of Sandbox' favorite resources for Autism
The Analog Brain Tool

Do you ever freeze up when there’s something you want or need to do? Struggle to initiate a task for reasons you don’t understand? The Analog Brain is a free to use interactive webpage designed to help people start tasks. It can also help you figure out what tasks might need to be done. The webpage doesn’t require a log in, but it will ask you to answer some questions about how you are feeling. It is designed to address a variety of health needs, comforts, and tasks of daily life

Question: Why are there no resources from Autism Speaks on Sandbox?
