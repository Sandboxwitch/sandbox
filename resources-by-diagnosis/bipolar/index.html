The International Bipolar Foundation
The international Bipolar Foundation is staffed by people with Bipolar experiences and provides resources for people with bipolar experiences. They have educational materials on bipolar definitions, treatment, and health management. The information is sorted for people who are newly diagnosed, and people who have known their diagnosis for awhile. They also have resources for both youth and adult bipolar experiences

The Depression and Bipolar Support Alliance

The Depression and Bipolar Support Alliance provides supports and resources to people of all ages  with depression or bipolar symptoms. Their resources include:

  • Crisis-Prevention toolkit of wellness, awareness, and calming tasks 
  • who have depression or bipolar symptoms
  • Supports for Teens  who are learning to understand, self-advocate, and manage their symptoms
  • Connections to competent healthcare professionals who can assist
  • Connections to diverse and focused mental health support groups
  • Roadmaps for how to recover after episodic behaviors or self harm attempts

Hearing Voices Network
Global support network and resources for people who experience psychosis, run by people who experience psychosis. Everyone is welcome regardless of diagnosis. This community is explicitly anti-psychiatry and anti-corrective, they simple want people to feel safe and happy in their neurodiverse identities.

Sane 

1800 187 263

Sane is a non-corrective support network for people with psychosis, by people with psychosis. They offer lots of services, community groups, webinars, advice, and on-call support to people managing psychosis. They have a helpful section just for bipolar experiences.
