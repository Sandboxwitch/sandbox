The Analog Brain Tool

Do you ever freeze up when there’s something you want or need to do? Struggle to initiate a task for reasons you don’t understand? The Analog Brain is a free to use interactive webpage designed to help people start tasks. It can also help you figure out what tasks might need to be done. The webpage doesn’t require a log in, but it will ask you to answer some questions about how you are feeling. It is designed to address a variety of health needs, comforts, and tasks of daily life

You Mean I'm Not Lazy, Stupid or Crazy?!

Author: Katie Kelly & Peggy Ramundo

An expansive self-help book for understanding and managing your ADD in a variety of contexts including school, work, life, hobbies, and parenting. Some of the information in the 2006 version is a little dated but there are still some effective tips, coping skills, and relatable stories to be found here

Self Care for People with ADHD

Author: Sasha Hamdani

This book focuses on ADHD affirming self care practices. It has practical advice on practicing self acceptance, while finding ways to understand and manage things that are difficult and frustrating.
