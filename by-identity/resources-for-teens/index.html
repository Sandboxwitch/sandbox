Resources for Teens

These resources have been written for teens trying to self-manage their disability advocacy. While anyone is welcome to any resource on sandbox, it is a fact that teens have different rights, different systems, and different bodies than adults. These resources are written specifically for teens who are learning to self advocate and managing their transition into adulthood

Advocating for Myself

The Pacer Center has a self advocacy resource library of PDFs written specifically for teen and young adult contexts; Topics include:
  • Navigating a 504 or IEP
  • Advocating as a new adult in college
  • Making the transition to independent living
  • Making a self advocacy checklist
  • What caring for yourself means

Kids Health: Teens Take Charge
The Nemours Foundation publishes health resources written for teen audiences. They offer advice on how to talk to the adults in your life about wanting more agency over your health, body, and disability management.

The Depression and Bipolar Support Alliance for Young Adults

The Depression and Bipolar Support Alliance provides supports and resources to people of all ages  with depression or bipolar symptoms. Their resources include:

  • Young adult guides for people who are learning to understand, self-advocate, and self-manage their symptoms
  • Crisis-Prevention toolkit of wellness, awareness, and calming tasks 
  • who have depression or bipolar symptoms
  • Connections to diverse and focused mental health support groups
  • Roadmaps for how to recover after episodic behaviors or self harm attempts

The International Bipolar Foundation
The international Bipolar Foundation is staffed by people with Bipolar experiences and provides resources for people with bipolar experiences. They have educational materials specifically for young adults on bipolar definitions, treatment, and health management. The information is sorted for people who are newly diagnosed, and people who have known their diagnosis for awhile.

Deafverse
The National Deaf Center has designed an in-browser chose-your-own-adventure video game for teens learning Deaf self advocacy and independence skills. Lessons are presented entirely in ASL, though subtitles are available in English.

Scarleteen's Guides to Safe Disabled Sex
Scarleteen is an education outreach group that has been running for over twenty years to provide safe, accurate, and easy to understand information on sexual health. They cater specifically to teen audiences, though anyone can use their information. They have resources on sexual health that are disability affirming, gender affirming, and relationship affirming.
