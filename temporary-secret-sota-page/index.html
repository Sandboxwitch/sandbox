Please be aware that its under construction, so there will be development notes and wonky links.  I wanted SOTA members to have the chance to see what SANDbox is as a concept, and what y'all would be adopting and working with in a little more detail.

Here is the script of concepts I plan to communicate to SOTA during the meeting today: 

A major part of my capstone is the development of a specialty resource library. 

It’s called SANDbox because its the Self Advocacy Network for Disability box.

Most of it is an online resource library but it has a physical manifestation. I think most people are aware of the concept of a little library, this is a lot like that but specialized, as many healthcare concepts are. 

I would like to ask SOTA to adopt this little library into a forever home on the health campus, or the OT floor somewhere. 

The "where" is open for discussion. Full disclosure: I don’t know what wishes or powers y'all have. This seemed better than going to Sarah and I thought you might legitamately be excited to be involved since y'all are bb OTs like me and community help projects are like catnip to bb OTs

Maybe the library box stays on the OT floor, maybe it goes in the Atrium. I’m open to suggestions

In adopting the library I am asking SOTA to manage some of its future and likely minimal upkeep

  • replacing a book once in awhile if it gets beat up or the information becomes harmfully outdated
  • restocking some of the stim toys and mini AT options if they go missing
  • opening the library door and spritzing everything with lysol during flu seasons once in awhile
  • removing trash from the library as people have an unfortunate tendency to shove trash in these things

I also imagine this being the sort of resource that SOTA might want to add new materials to themselves. I know my class was frequently talking about books, podcasts, ect

The goal of this library is to increase a sense of agency in disabled people. Disabled students would benefit, but it could also serve as a resource to help bb OTs explore  the responsibility of promoting client self advocacy.

My capstone cites a study that says over 60% of OTs felt like they didn’t know how to teach patient self advocacy skills and this could serve as an important resource. Thats not a good stat, maybe this box could also help us bb OTs decrease that yucky stat.
